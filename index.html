Group Reflection

Here is our group reflection

Nguyen Thanh Vinh

Throughout the group work, our biggest advantage was that we came from very different backgrounds, so every team member could offer distinct perspectives and input to the group work. However, this also set a challenge as the individual expertise was too diverged, there are areas where some members are very experienced while others may struggle. Thus, we follow an individualistic way of teamwork, where we divide the work by the individual's key ability. The team meetings, held weekly, are primarily for updates on each member's progress and how we could support each other.
The most surprising part to me was how our individual ability could complement each other to deliver a good result, most notably in our second assignment. For example, I was in charge of the business aspects of the proposal since I am the only business-major in my group, while the other 3 worked on the technical sides (technical writing, programming, web design, etc.). The part I believed could have been improved was in our communication and planning. Each member worked individually so there was a difference in the individual pace, while there are certain parts that depend on the others to be done (e.g: the team profile has to be written first so the web could be started). Thus, the key learning here is firstly, to carry out the best result, we should develop a group plan that utilises everyone's individual capability to the fullest. However, we should set the priority on individual tasks in more details so everyone could finish their tasks without waiting for the delays of others.
